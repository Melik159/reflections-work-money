# Purpose of the Articles Directory

This directory contains articles, empirical notes, methodological essays, and supporting material developed from the economic corpus of **Pierre Campergue**.

The purpose of these publications is not to appropriate, simplify, or prematurely validate the corpus. It is to make selected questions clearer, testable, readable, and open to criticism.

## Intellectual source

The starting point is Pierre Campergue’s manuscript:

> *Reflections on the Economic Power of Work and the Mechanical Power of Money*

Campergue’s work is treated here as an intellectual corpus requiring careful reading, clarification, reconstruction, and, where possible, empirical confrontation.

The central concern is not to reduce economics to thermodynamics. The objective is to explore the formal discipline proposed by the corpus: precise definitions, explicit variables, distinction between monetary signs and purchasing power, distinction between labour and economic Work, and distinction between accounting flows and productive transformations.

## Aim of the articles

The articles in this directory aim to:

- clarify concepts introduced or implied in Pierre Campergue’s corpus;
- distinguish what is conceptual, empirical, methodological, or speculative;
- formulate research questions in a precise and falsifiable way where possible;
- connect the corpus to available data without overstating the results;
- publish working papers, notes, or preprints when the material is mature enough;
- provide code, datasets, scripts, and reproducibility material for review;
- invite criticism, correction, and technical discussion.

## Methodological position

The working method is deliberately iterative:

```text
think
formulate
test
verify
criticize
correct
repeat
````

The repository is not meant to freeze a doctrine. It is meant to support a research process.

Claims should therefore be separated according to their status:

```text
observed
computed
reproduced
conceptual
hypothetical
uncertain
open
```

Whenever possible, articles should make clear:

* what is directly stated in Campergue’s manuscript;
* what is an interpretation by the repository maintainer or article author;
* what is supported by data;
* what is merely suggested by the data;
* what remains unresolved.

## Role of code

Code included in or linked from the articles is intended to make claims inspectable.

The purpose of the code is to allow readers to:

* reproduce figures and tables;
* verify transformations applied to the data;
* inspect assumptions;
* modify parameters;
* detect mistakes;
* propose corrections;
* compare alternative data sources or models.

Code is not presented as proof by itself. It is a tool for reproducibility, criticism, and correction.

## Role of empirical work

Empirical analyses developed from the corpus should be read as limited tests of specific claims or implications.

They do not validate the entire corpus.

For example, an empirical note on GDP-energy coupling may support a limited macroeconomic observation compatible with Campergue’s framework, without proving the full Work-to-Money formalism.

The preferred wording is:

> empirical support for a limited implication

not:

> proof of the whole theory

## Publication policy

Material in this directory may include:

* working papers;
* empirical notes;
* methodological notes;
* article drafts;
* preprint-ready versions;
* replication packages;
* response notes after criticism or review.

A document may be updated when errors are found, when better data become available, or when the argument is clarified.

Publication is understood as part of a scientific process, not as a claim of finality.

## Scientific attitude

The intended attitude is:

* preserve the original corpus accurately;
* clarify before defending;
* test before asserting;
* state uncertainty explicitly;
* distinguish evidence from interpretation;
* correct mistakes publicly when found;
* prefer reproducibility over rhetorical strength;
* leave open questions open.

The objective is to contribute to knowledge by making the corpus readable, testable, and discussable.

## Authorship and responsibility

Pierre Campergue remains the intellectual source of the preserved corpus.

Articles written from, about, or around the corpus must identify their own author and must not blur the distinction between:

* Pierre Campergue’s original text;
* later interpretation;
* empirical extensions;
* code-based analyses;
* editorial framing.

Unless otherwise stated, responsibility for articles, code, interpretations, mistakes, and updates belongs to their respective authors.

## Summary

This directory exists to transform a preserved corpus into a living research program.

The goal is not to claim certainty.

The goal is to clarify fields of knowledge, publish when possible, provide code for review, and apply a scientific method:

```text
think, formulate, verify, correct, and begin again.
```
